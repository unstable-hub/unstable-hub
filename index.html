Series Overview

What is Unstable Universe?

Unstable Universe (also called Unstable SMP) is a scripted hardcore Minecraft video series created by ParrotX2, Wemmbu, SpokeIsHere, and FlameFrags. It premiered on April 21, 2024 with SpokeIsHere's episode "How to Destroy the Unstable SMP." Season 1 ran April 21 2024 – January 30 2025 (27 episodes). Season 2 ran February 2 2025 – February 16 2026 (88+ episodes, introduced FlameFrags as fourth protagonist). Seasons were then abolished in favor of continuous storytelling, with Season 3 beginning shortly after.

The series uses one-life hardcore mechanics where death carries permanent in-universe consequences. Players build civilizations, forge alliances, betray enemies, and fight wars — all while a web of background mysteries ties every arc together. Each creator films from their own perspective, meaning the same event often looks completely different depending on who you watch.

The series is notable for its overlapping POV storytelling, long-running mysteries (The Forge, JamatoP, the Underworld, NULL Players), and the way its writers use vanilla Minecraft mechanics to create genuinely dramatic moments. As of April 2026, the wiki has 16,426 edits across 650 articles.

The Exploit Arc

The very beginning. Spoke exploits the server's item duplication system, meets the mysterious JamatoP, and funds a massive construction project as cover. Every mystery in Unstable Universe traces back to this arc.

Primary

Season
Season 1 Opening

Spoke discovers a shulker-box item duplication exploit and immediately begins abusing it. He hires builder TheRealSquiddo using exploited items to construct The Wonders — elaborate server structures that serve as cover for the operation. Meanwhile, he encounters JamatoP: a player with full-protection netherite armor carrying enchantments that simply should not exist in vanilla Minecraft. JamatoP grants Spoke access to the gear. Wemmbu spots the transfer happening and confronts Spoke — who lies convincingly.

Spoke then builds a giant vault using exploited items, luring Wemmbu, Parrot, and others into trying to break in. The final Wonder is destroyed. Spoke chunk-bans himself to escape the fallout.

  • JamatoP's impossible armor is the origin of every server mystery — it implies admin access or a pre-existing exploit older than the server
  • The Wonders were paid for entirely with illegally duplicated items — the server's economy was distorted from Day 1

  • Parrot and Wifies completed Spoke's elaborate fake scavenger hunt — Wifies permanently chunk-banned himself for nothing. Spoke's "great secret" was always empty revenge
  • Ro working with Wemmbu against Parrot plants the seeds of their complicated future relationship

The PrinceZam Empire Arc

The server's first true civilisation war. PrinceZam builds a spawn-controlling empire, employs FlameFrags as enforcer, tasks Wemmbu with guarding an empty vault, and hires ClownPierce when things go wrong. Everyone gets betrayed by everyone else.

PrinceZam establishes the first dominant empire — walls around spawn, a giant PrinceZam statue (demolished by Wemmbu within hours), and an obsidian anti-orbital roof. He tasks Wemmbu with building a colony to guard a "secret vault" — which was always empty. Wemmbu spent episodes guarding nothing on Zam's orders, and Zam knew. After Wifies gets chunk-banned, Parrot breaks him out of Zam's prison with help from Rekrap2, also freeing Derapchu, Fantst, and Jumper.

Zam hires ClownPierce to assassinate Parrot and Spoke. He also orders Flame to fake-betray the Empire and attack Wemmbu — a loyalty test Wemmbu never knew about. ManePear, disgusted, secretly works against the Empire and helps Wemmbu escape. In the war's climax, Wemmbu assembles a war coalition, captures three key Zam officers (Yungwillx, Peentar, Sorhin) through traps and blackmail, rescues Eggchan, and unleashes his orbital strike cannon. Zam and Wemmbu duel — Zam finally apologizes. ClownPierce then arrives uninvited and kidnaps both of them for Proton Prison.

  • The empty vault reveal is the series's most satisfying anti-climax — Wemmbu spent the entire early arc guarding worthless air
  • ManePear's mid-duel armor removal (refusing to fight Flame with broken kit) seeds Flame's entire honor philosophy
  • Flame was secretly ordered by Zam to betray the Empire and attack Wemmbu — a staged test that Wemmbu never discovered
  • Wemmbu fired his orbital cannon from inside the Empire's walls, causing a server lag spike that bought his escape
  • The Zam Empire's creation moment echoes on infinite loop in the Far Lands — its history preserved forever

The dominant antagonist arc of Season 1. Ashswag's criminal organisation uses invisibility potions, stasis chambers over the void, and an armor-trim hierarchy to control the server. The Mafia used armor trims as rank — copper, gold, diamond, with ClownPierce's unique netherite above all. Members operated under invisibility, communicated via colored fireworks, and were controlled by stasis chambers: each member's ender pearl floated over the void, and any diamond player could pull it at any time. Flame was the only diamond member permitted to refuse his mace. Ashswag held a dead-man's switch: his death would trigger all chambers simultaneously.

Parrot builds an End Civilization (found by Mafia agent Deanthebean9, who is then freed) and a Far Lands Civilization (which the Mafia destroys with TNT bombers, killing Dean). Horace Altman and five survivors found District 13. Spoke infiltrates the Mafia as a double agent, meeting Ashswag and receiving the warning: "You'll turn out exactly like me." Wifies volunteers to join the Mafia as a spy to protect Parrot — Ashswag pulls his stasis within hours. Parrot lights beacons across the entire server as both a memorial and a tactical distraction. Mafia members drink milk, removing invisibility, exposing the stasis guard. Spoke decodes the "Mafia Eyes" book as a maze map. All protagonists storm the spawn prison via end portal. Stasis chambers are destroyed — all Mafia die simultaneously. Spoke finds Ashswag in the Far Lands and chooses not to kill him.

  • MinuteTech confirmed: "The Mafia used to have a different, less cruel leader" — who was before Ashswag?
  • Wifies's death is Season 1's most impactful moment — he volunteered knowing the risk
  • Beacon plan served dual purpose: memorial for Wifies AND made Mafia members drink milk, revealing their invisibility
  • Flame was the only Mafia diamond member to refuse his mace — Ashswag made a special exception due to his combat skill
  • Members could not carry Totems — a popping totem would reveal their invisibility and mean death

The Power Arc

Wemmbu is hunted by an invisible assassin (WyLL), raids BAT headquarters, explores the Far Lands with Eggchan, discovers a city near The Forge, defeats Lakerek who wields Forge-level items, destroys a stash of maces, and comes face to face with the server's deepest mystery — The Forge itself.

Wemmbu raids BAT Headquarters and is immediately jumped by players and an invisible assassin. He tracks one player back to their base and obtains coords to WyLL's identity via a dropped compass. WyLL offers to stop attacking if Wemmbu kills one final target — Wemmbu does, but WyLL betrays him and traps him over the void. Wemmbu escapes through a void hole at BAT HQ. He returns and kills most of WyLL's team, nearly killing WyLL who stasises out. He then helps Loppezz clear Ice Mountain Civilization — but stops when the mountain is revealed to be rigged to blow if pushed too far.

Wemmbu and Eggchan journey into the Far Lands seeking The Forge. They find infinite looping dungeon rooms containing 64+ Enchanted Golden Apples. They discover a city everyone believes to be The Forge — but locals say it isn't. Egg finds a compass leading back to a Far Lands orbital strike cannon array used by Lakerek. Lakerek's friends reveal The Forge is actually back at base. Wemmbu is presented with an End portal — Egg jumps in front and is shot in by an arrow. Three types of Far Lands phenomena: (1) Infinite loops — existing structures (man-made or natural) repeat endlessly. Wemmbu looted 64+ Enchanted Golden Apples from one infinite dungeon loop. His own orbital strike cannons repeat infinitely — player-built structures should not loop; something is copying them. (2) Historical echoes — the Zam Empire's creation plays on loop; OG players vs Elder Guardian replays; Burgle5 starving to death echoes in the Spikelands. (3) The portal anomaly — Nether portals at Far Lands coordinates lead to the Underworld, not the Nether, allowing rapid traversal controlled by those who know the routes.
